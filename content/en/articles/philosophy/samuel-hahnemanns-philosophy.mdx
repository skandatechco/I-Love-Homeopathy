PHILOSOPHY: SAMUEL HAHNEMANN
"The fiend of health by S. Hahnemann Part I
In old-women's philosophy there is something good, if we
only know where to find it." Sometimes, we, who belong
to the inferior class of untranscendental doctors, are too dull
to perceive, because, alas, we are not endowed with the
super-subtle sixth or perhaps seventh sense, were it otherwise
we might see the great importance of the triple and crucial point
more especially if the excessively lucky precaution is observed of
commencing and carrying out the operation from beginning to
end without speaking a word, which indeed it were almost too
much to expect from an ordinary woman. Observation of what
old women have been doing from generation to generation sometimes is a principle of cure that we have to perceive.
