### **Writing Fiction – Tips from Top Writers**

Someone has once said that writing is a solitary confinement and if you are not ready for it, you shouldn’t apply. We at [termpaper.help](https://termpaper.help) believe it to be absolutely true. The amount of hesitation and self-doubt that a writer lives through cannot be compared to any other profession.
To make it just a little bit easier on you, we have asked our top writers to share the secrets they have for writing productively. Most of them work on fiction novels in their free time and thus, could give some advice to aspiring fiction writers.
**Tip No. 1**: Introduce your characters and settings in the first third of the novel and start building the tension in the second one. You shouldn’t know what will happen at the end – the novel has to take you there with the development of events. This is the only way to avoid artificial parts in the plot and understand what your characters would actually do.
**Tip No. 2**: Show, don’t tell. A mistake that many aspiring writers make is telling the reader what happened or was happening directly. Writing is still an art and should not be taken just as a tool to transfer information. That’s why you shouldn’t tell your readers that the moon was shining. Instead, tell them about the bright reflection in puddles. Everything that happens can be described through the effect it has on its surroundings. The picture you paint will force readers to deduct, which is a natural process for high-quality writing.
**Tip No.** 3:Count your exclamation points. An exclamation point is an instrument that should be used with care and precision. Use one too many, and all of them will lose their power. Use too few, and you will underperform in terms of expression. A good rule of thumb is this: when you are tempted to end a sentence with an exclamation point, first look at the sentence without it. If the exclamation point is all that adds expression to it, then your sentence is not very strong in the first place, and the exclamation point won’t help it.
**Tip No. 4**: First learn the classic rules of writing, then learn to break them. Every new school of writing was developed on the basis of previous ones by people who knew well what boundaries they wanted to step over. The point is, your writing rebellion will only be effective and justified if you have solid background and know what you are doing. Amateur attempts at original writing forms are always seen for what they are – amateur attempts.
**Tip No. 5**: Accept that you will never like your own writing, at least not as much as you would want to. You might have a range of feelings, starting from relief and up to shame, but there is no point of complete satisfaction and complacency in there. The good news is, it means you are good. The bad news is, you’ll have to learn to put up with this feeling.
