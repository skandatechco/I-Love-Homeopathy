Skin care products come in various brands and price tags; some are very expensive, and some are affordable and still effective in their performance. However, there are some home remedies for skin problems that do not require a shopping basket.
The home remedies for skin problems we are going to look at are not only DIY alternatives; they are fun and simple to produce. Below are some homemade products for some common skin issues.
**Calm acne**
Acne is what we call an unexpected neighbor because it usually pops up at unexpected times. Worst of all, we happen to see this skin reaction before heading out for a date or a party. This process is also on the list for [home remedies for sebaceous cysts](https://homeremedybook.com/home-remedies-for-sebaceous-cyst/).
To create a home-based acne treatment, you need to have a simple washcloth that is already soaked in warm water and follow the steps below.
Hold the washcloth on the pimple for 5 minutes as you compress it for the warm feeling to maintain on the skin. Avoid using hot water because it will not only burn you; it won't treat your acne.
Compress the washcloth again for another 2 hours until our skin is clear from the acne. You will notice the skin getting clearer after a period of 24 to 36 hours.
**Unclogging blockheads**
Most of our faces have blackheads. To help your skin get free from them, you need to mix some rose water and oatmeal to make some soft paste. Once the mixture is well prepared, apply it to the areas where the [blackheads](http://ilovehomoeopathy.com/glossary/blackheads/) are and rub it in small circular movements.
Leave the paste glued to your skin for approximately 20 minutes. Rinse the paste off your face. Use the oatmeal mask anytime to have a radiant face glow.
**Cleans and exfoliate**
To get cost-effective and great working skincare cleanser and exfoliator, create a solution of a tablespoon of sugar and a teaspoon of water. Open the pores by using a warm cloth for a few minutes over the face before applying the solution.
Rub the solution of water and sugar on your skin for a minute before you rinse. You will end up having a clear, clean and refreshed skin. To get a more exfoliated mixture, use sea salt instead of sugar. You can use the mixture on your entire body as it counters the rough spots that are usually on the feet, elbows, and knees.
Salt has antiseptic properties, making it an anti-inflammatory agent to be used for medicinal benefits on the skin, but don’t overdo it. Once a week, use the occasional exfoliation mixture.
**Alleviate dry skin**
For this treatment, you will need a bottle of olive oil. Olive oil is claimed to have been was used in ancient civilization to moisturize the skin, and it is also a great skin care product today. The good thing is that almost every kind of vegetable oil can work for your skin.
Use a palm-sized amount of oil on a daily basis after taking a bath. Gently spread the oil on your hands and apply it on the dry spots on your skin.
**Nourish**
To lighten and brighten your skin, homemade honey mask can do the trick. For this to happen, you need to beat an egg yolk in a bowl, add one teaspoon of olive oil and blend to mix. Add a tablespoon of honey which has been warmed up by hot water and blend the mixture.
Apply the honey mask to your face and avoid the eye area. Let it stay on your face for half an hour and rinse it off afterward. You will easily notice an improvement on your skin just about that time.
**Hydrate**
Drink approximately 8 glasses of water each day. The benefits of drinking water not only aids in better skin, but also contributes on overall health.
 
**Tone and tighten**
You can easily get witch hazel from your local drug store to use in this process. Witch hazel is the main component in most of the toners, and it is amazing when applied on its own. Use a cotton ball to apply it after washing and drying the face. In case you have a dry face, dilute witch hazel into two portions of water.
Witch hazel is an astringent which always has to be moisturized after toning. To get a more refreshing toner, mix two tablespoons of witch hazel with a tablespoon of rose water and a teaspoon of lemon juice.
Ensure that you stock enough witch hazel at home, because it also promotes quick healing on bumps and bruises, apart from tightening and toning your skin.
**Moisturize**
Moisturizing is essential to skin care product for your skin because it locks hydration in the body and it creates a layer of protection between the natural elements and your skin. To make an effective moisturizer at home, almond oil is claimed to have skin care capabilities. For this treatment, place six drops of almond oil and a few drops of water on your palms. Mix them and rub then smoothly on your skin.
**Soothe**
You might find your skin turning red or irritated on occasion, and countering this issue is as easy as using a glass of milk. Get the job done with milk from any farm animal. Use a washcloth in this process. Soak the washcloth in a glass of milk that is at room temperature and gently press it on the irritated area for approximately five minutes.
Repeat this process often depending on the irritation you feel on your skin. The purpose of milk is to soothe, soften and nourish the skin to promote healing.
**Minimize Pores**
The pores on our skin are open targets that can easily get clogged, which finally makes them appear larger than normal. To reduce this effect from home, run hot water in a blocked sink or in a large bowl, then use a towel to cover your head and lean over for the steam to get in for about 3 minutes. Gently rinse or exfoliate the skin to minimize the clogged pores, and pat the skin dry, and then use a toner to refine and tighten the skin.
**Conclusion**
The natural remedies for skin problems we have looked at are excellent skin care treatments that work well when your skin has mild infections. However, if you have wildly infected skin conditions, contact your medical doctor get the appropriate help. But in case you want to make your skin look alive again, try out these ideas and get your shine on.
