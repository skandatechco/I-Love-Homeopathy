HISTORY: Please meet Dr. Charles Sumner (1821-1888), he graduated from the New York Medical College and the Hahnemann Medical College of Philadelphia. Dr. Sumner was a leading voice in the debate between the two schools of medicine: Allopathic vs. Homeopathic.
He was a member of the committee of homeopathic physicians tasked with deciding the matter of establishing the Homeopathic Hospital. Although Dr. Sumner died on May 5th of 1888 and didn't live to see the opening of the Rochester Homeopathic Hospital in 1889, his respected voice in the debate of its creation was instrumental in its success!
