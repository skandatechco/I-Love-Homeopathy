**Benoît Jules Mure** (May 15, 1809, [Lyon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyon "Lyon") — March 4, 1858, [Cairo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo "Cairo")) was a French [homeopath](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homeopathy "Homeopathy"), [naturalist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naturalist "Naturalist"), and [anarcho-communist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anarchist_communism "Anarchist communism").
After his studies in medicine at the [University of Montpellier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Montpellier "University of Montpellier"), which he never finished, he travelled throughout Europe, and spent time in [Sicily](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sicily "Sicily") trying to cure his [tuberculosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuberculosis "Tuberculosis"). In his search for a cure, he became an adept of homeopathy, which he practiced in such places as Sicily and [Malta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malta "Malta"), before settling in [Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil "Brazil") in 1840. He attempted to expand his activities in 1842, by creating an Institute in [Saí](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Sa%C3%AD&action=edit&redlink=1 "Saí (page does not exist)"), [Santa Catarina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Catarina_(state) "Santa Catarina (state)") (*Instituto Homeopático de Saí*) training locals in homeopathy, which he saw as a weapon in combatting the [endemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endemic_(epidemiology) "Endemic (epidemiology)") diseases within underprivileged communities, especially the [Afro-Brazilian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afro-Brazilian "Afro-Brazilian") one.
Inspired by the [utopian socialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utopian_socialism "Utopian socialism") of [Charles Fourier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Fourier "Charles Fourier"), Mure also created a [Phalanstère](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phalanst%C3%A8re "Phalanstère") near [São Francisco do Sul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Francisco_do_Sul "São Francisco do Sul") (*Falanstério do Saí* or *Colônia Industrial do Saí*), which expanded to surrounding areas such as [Vila da Glória](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vila_da_Gl%C3%B3ria&action=edit&redlink=1 "Vila da Glória (page does not exist)") (*Colônia do Palmital*). He received backing from [Antero Ferreira de Brito](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Antero_Ferreira_de_Brito&action=edit&redlink=1 "Antero Ferreira de Brito (page does not exist)") and the rest of Santa Catarina's government of the period, but the project was not to survive.
He left for [Rio de Janeiro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro "Rio de Janeiro") in 1843, and founded the *Instituto Homeopático do Brasil*, serving as its president until 1848 (when he returned to Europe). In 1852, he started homeopathical activities in Cairo, [Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexandria "Alexandria"), and other areas of [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt_under_Muhammad_Ali_and_his_successors "Egypt under Muhammad Ali and his successors") (including *[Turkiyah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkiyah "Turkiyah")* [Sudan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan "Sudan")). He died there six years later.
Literature references: Benoit Mure missionnaire de l'homéopathie. 1809-1858, Roseline Brillat, Editions Boiron S.A, 1988.
