Adolph., the haughty man
			Despised this world and had a plan
			To dissipate his gathering gloom
			By bagging lots of lebensraum ;
			And instead of bread and stilton
			Axminster was his food ( or Wilton)
			And nation suffered after nation
			From Adolph's gastric fermentation ;
			And in his car he'd stand upright
			Because his thighs were feeling tight,
			Or else because he thought to ride
		Like this enhanced his wondrous pride.
